# Elena — Engineering manager

**Profile:** Manages 8 front-end engineers. Lives in Google Calendar, Slack, Linear. 30-second
patience budget, half the day in meetings, uses tools between meetings on her phone.

## Clarity — Yes (barely)
Within 5 seconds I read "INTERACTION PRINCIPLES / interactive lessons in how interfaces feel"
plus a numbered 8-lesson sidebar (direct manipulation, springs vs easing, frame budget...).
That's enough to know it's an interactive course on interaction-design craft for people who
build UI. What slowed me: the giant H1 is the *lesson title* ("content moves with your finger.")
not a "what is this / who's it for" headline — for half a beat I thought I'd landed mid-article.
The subhead in the top-left header is what saved it. So: clear, but the page leads with a lesson
instead of a one-line "who this is for."

## Value — No (for me personally)
This is well-made — the drag demo tracks 1:1, gain/latency sliders update a live status readout,
lesson 4 lets you toggle anticipation/follow-through/squash & stretch independently. It's the
real thing, not a blog post. But I am the wrong user. I don't ship UI; I manage people who do.
What I do *today* with content like this: nothing — I don't sit and walk an 8-lesson curriculum.
My actual move would be to drop a link in Slack to a report whose animations feel janky. For
that I need (a) a way to deep-link a single lesson ("hey, lesson 6, frame budget"), and (b) a
30-second "why this matters" payoff I can vouch for before forwarding. The lessons are good but
this is a sit-down learning tool, and my reports are also busy — an 8-chapter interactive
curriculum is a "I'll get to it" tab that never gets gotten to. It doesn't save me or my team
time in any session I actually have.

## Advocacy — 4/10
Quality is high and I'd believe a designer or a junior FE eng could genuinely level up here, so
it's not a 1 or 2. But I would not bring this up unprompted, and the realistic moment I'd share
it (forwarding one lesson to one report) isn't well supported: no obvious per-lesson share link
surfaced, and there's no skimmable "TL;DR what good feels like" I could paste so they'd open it.
It's a learning destination, not something that slots into my Slack/Linear/Calendar workflow.
Honest weak fit — I'd bookmark it and forget it.

## Top complaints
1. Leads with a lesson title, not a "who this is for / why bother" line — costs me clarity-confidence.
2. No surfaced per-lesson share/deep-link so I could forward exactly one lesson to a report.
3. Time mismatch: 8-lesson interactive curriculum vs. a manager (and her reports) with no sit-down time.
