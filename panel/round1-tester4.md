# Tomás — Operations analyst

Device: corporate Windows laptop, Edge. Stack: Excel (power user), Tableau, Jira, Teams, SharePoint.

## Clarity — Yes
Within 5 seconds I knew exactly what this is. The masthead reads "INTERACTION PRINCIPLES /
interactive lessons in how interfaces feel," and the left rail lists 8 numbered lessons
(direct manipulation, springs vs easing, frame budget, etc.). Lesson 1 opens with a big
"content moves with your finger." headline and a draggable square labeled "← DRAG ME." No
ambiguity: this teaches interaction/animation design, hands-on. I'd tell a friend: "It's a
free, no-login mini-course that lets you fiddle with sliders to feel why UI animations feel
good or janky — clearly aimed at UI/UX and motion designers." The "WHY IT MATTERS FOR YOUR
PORTFOLIO" notes on each lesson make the intended audience explicit.

## Value — No
This is an honest non-fit for me. I build spreadsheets in Excel and dashboards in Tableau;
I never craft UI animation, springs, or frame budgets. Nothing here maps to a task in my
week. I dragged the box in Lesson 1 (gain/latency controls work, status row updates live),
poked the "INJECT WORK" FPS slider in Lesson 6, fired the animation-principle buttons in
Lesson 4 — all genuinely well made and responsive. But "tasteful, not Disney" and "reason
about rAF costs" is language for a different profession. Today I solve my problems in Excel
and Tableau; this saves me zero time because the problem it solves isn't one I have. The
browser-tool, no-install, no-signup angle appeals to me abstractly (IT blocks installs, so
I like that it just runs in Edge) — but appeal-in-principle isn't use.

## Advocacy — 2/10
I won't bring this up to my ops peers; none of them do interaction design either, so it
would land as noise. The only reason it's not a 1 is that it's genuinely polished and
I could forward the URL to exactly one person — a front-end/UX friend — with a "you might
like this." That's a single, prompted, niche referral, not advocacy. For my own world it's
a 0. Nothing is broken; it's simply not for me, and I'm scoring fit, not craft.
