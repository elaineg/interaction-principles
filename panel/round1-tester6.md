# Jules — Content & community marketer

**Tech:** medium · **Device:** 50/50 desktop+mobile · **Stack:** Notion, Buffer, Mailchimp, Figma (consuming), X/LinkedIn/Mastodon, Discord

## Clarity — Yes
Within 5 seconds I knew exactly what this is. "INTERACTION PRINCIPLES / interactive lessons in how interfaces feel" up top, an "08 LESSONS" badge, a numbered lesson rail down the left, and a giant "content moves with your finger." with a literal DRAG ME box. I'd tell a friend: "It's a free, no-login interactive course on why good UI *feels* good — you drag stuff and tweak sliders to watch animations go from buttery to janky." The lesson titles (springs vs easing, frame budget, interruptibility) are the only mildly insidery words, but the demos explain themselves. Genuinely the clearest cold-open I've hit.

## Value — No (for ME)
The build is excellent and I respect it. Lesson 2's side-by-side SPRING vs EASING with a "FIRE BOTH" button and a draggable cubic-bézier curve, and Lesson 6's real FPS meter with an "INJECT WORK" slider that actually drops frames — these are *real*, not faked. As a craft object it's a 9.

But this is squarely for people who BUILD interfaces. My day is Buffer scheduling, Mailchimp sends, Notion content calendars, and Discord. I consume Figma, I don't ship motion specs, I have no portfolio, and I will literally never sit down to "tune a spring's damping." Every lesson ends with "WHY IT MATTERS FOR YOUR PORTFOLIO" — which is a polite way of telling me I'm not the audience. Nothing here saves me time on anything I actually do. I won't reach for it again.

One honest nit even as a layperson: at GAIN 2× with 0ms latency the STATUS reads "degraded" in red — I read "degraded" as lag, so a zero-latency "degraded" briefly confused me before I realized it meant gain≠1.

## Advocacy — 4/10
Not a 7-to-be-nice. It's a 4 because there IS a real path: this is share-worthy "cool explainer" content I might drop in a designer Discord or quote-tweet as "look how nicely someone taught springs vs easing." That's a one-time share, not a recommendation — I'd never tell a marketing peer "you need this," because none of us do. The portfolio framing and parameter-tuning depth cap it firmly below "recommend." Great for an engineer/designer panelist; weak fit for me, exactly as expected.
